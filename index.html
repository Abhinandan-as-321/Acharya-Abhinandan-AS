Karnataka

Karnataka, a state located in the southern part of India, has a rich culinary tradition that reflects its diverse culture and geography. The cuisine of Karnataka is known for its unique flavors, use of local ingredients, and variety.Dishes like Bisi Bele Bath and unique breads like Ragi Rotti. It features tangy curries like Sambhar and sweet treats like Mysore Pak.

Kerala

Kerala's cuisine is renowned for its aromatic spices and coconut-based dishes. Sadya, a traditional feast served on banana leaves, highlights an array of vegetarian delights. Fish, especially in dishes like Meen Curry, is integral to coastal cuisine. Appam, a fluffy rice pancake, and Puttu, steamed rice cake, are staples.

Andhra Pradesh

Andhra Pradesh's cuisine is known for its fiery flavors and use of red and green chilies. Spicy pickles, tangy chutneys, and aromatic biryanis stand out. Signature dishes like Andhra Chicken Curry and Gongura Pachadi (sorrel leaf chutney) exemplify the state's vibrant palate. Rice-based meals with accompaniments like Pappu and Avakaya are common.

Delhi

Delhi's food scene is a tantalizing tapestry of flavors. Street food like "Chole Bhature," "Pani Puri," and "Kebabs" entice taste buds. Iconic dishes such as "Butter Chicken" and "Aloo Paratha" are celebrated. Rich Mughlai influence is seen in aromatic curries, while Old Delhi's bustling lanes offer a medley of delights. From traditional to modern fusion, Delhi's cuisine is very unique.

Punjab

Punjab's cuisine is a hearty celebration of robust flavors. Tandoori dishes like "Chicken Tikka," rich curries like "Palak Paneer," and indulgent "Makki di Roti" with "Sarson da Saag" define its essence. Generous use of ghee and spices brings warmth to the food. Punjab's culinary heritage is a blend of tradition and hospitality, portraying its agrarian roots with every flavorful bite.
